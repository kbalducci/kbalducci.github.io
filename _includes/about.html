Experience

    I completed my Bachelor's in Early Childhood Education and Spanish. I spent a semester abroad in Seville, Spain. Immediately following graduation, I enrolled in the M.Ed. program for Early Education. I completed the program in 2002.

  • 2002-2006

    Making a difference in the lives of students

    Immediately following graduation I worked as a 1:1 special education aide in a combined grade K/1 classroom in Wellesley, MA. The following year, I moved to the Concord Public Schools District. I spent 3 years in Concord as a Kindergarten and 2nd-grade teacher.

  • 2006-2009

    New city, new job!

    Following the 2006 school year, I moved to Austin in search of a warmer climate and an exciting new adventure. I used my educational expertise in my new role as an editor at Houghton Mifflin Harcourt Publishing Company.

  • 2009-2014

    Teaching in Texas!

    I felt my career as a teacher was not over yet and decided to head back into the classroom. I taught Kindergarten and then Technology Applications at Rowe Lane Elementary. I also served as the campus "Digital Literacy Lead" and pioneered the Computer Science Club. In 2014 I moved to Park Crest Middle School where I taught Video Broadcasting, Technology Applications and Web Design (Basic HTML) courses.

  • 2015

    All coding, all the time!

    I had always been interested in Technology and building things. This interest grew quickly as I dove more in depth in teaching different technologies. I became extemely interested in learning more about computer programming and how it can open up new doors for students. I enrolled in the Ruby on Rails program at The Iron Yard. I spent 60-80 hours per week for 12 weeks learning Ruby on Rails, HTML, CSS, SASS, GIT, Full-Stack Development, Postgresql, Javascript, TDD, BDD, Agile Methodologies and more. I developed new connections in the tech world and learned an incredible amount.
